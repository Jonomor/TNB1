Investors often look for "partnerships." Analysts look for "infrastructure." The XRPL's moat is built on the latter.

### Native vs. Layered Compliance

On Ethereum, compliance is an afterthought - typically added via smart contract wrappers or Layer 2 rollups. If the smart contract fails, the compliance fails.

On the XRPL, with amendments like **XLS-80** and **DID (Decentralized Identity)**, compliance is enforced at the protocol level. If a wallet does not hold the specific 'Credential' required by the Domain, the ledger literally *rejects the transaction* before it is processed.

This is "Hard Compliance," and it is the only type of compliance risk officers at JP Morgan or BNY Mellon will accept.

### The Integrated Workflow

This upgrade aligns perfectly with **ISO 20022** standards. The metadata capabilities of XRPL allow for rich data to travel with the payment, meaning the KYC attestation (Who sent this?) and the AML check (Is this clean?) happen atomically with the value transfer.

This creates a "Golden Record" of truth that legacy systems cannot replicate.
